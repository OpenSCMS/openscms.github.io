Linkage Authority (LA)

Overview

The Linkage Authority (LA) is a critical SCMS component responsible for generating and managing linkage values that enable privacy-preserving misbehavior resolution. The LA provides the linkage seeds and values used during authorization certificate generation, allowing authorized SCMS entities to link multiple pseudonym certificates to the same End Entity when necessary for security investigations, while preserving unlinkability during normal operations.

In the OpenSCMS architecture, the LA operates as an internal service that is invoked exclusively by the Registration Authority (RA) during authorization certificate provisioning flows. The LA does not expose any external APIs to V2X clients and does not participate directly in client-facing protocol interactions.

Role in Authorization Certificate Provisioning

During the authorization certificate provisioning flow, the RA queries the LA to obtain the linkage values required for a specific batch of authorization certificates. These linkage values are embedded into the certificates by the Authorization Certificate Authority (ACA) and enable privacy-preserving revocation and misbehavior detection.

The LA maintains linkage seeds and derives linkage values based on cryptographic parameters and time periods defined by SCMS policy. The linkage values are designed such that certificates from the same End Entity can be linked by authorized misbehavior investigators without revealing the EE's identity during normal V2X communication.

Security and Privacy Considerations

The LA is a highly sensitive component within the SCMS architecture. Compromise of the LA's cryptographic material or linkage seeds could enable unauthorized tracking of End Entities, undermining the privacy guarantees of the SCMS.

To minimize risk, the LA is designed as a narrowly scoped, stateless service with no external API exposure. All interactions with the LA occur through internal service-to-service calls initiated by the RA, and all linkage computations are performed using the shared cryptographic engine provided by oscms-codecs-bridge.

By isolating the LA behind the RA and limiting its responsibilities to linkage value generation, OpenSCMS ensures that the LA operates under strict architectural constraints, reducing the attack surface and facilitating security auditing.
